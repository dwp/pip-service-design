I just need to tell you about how Department for Communities (DfC) may ask other organisations to share your information

The DfC or approved healthcare professionals that work for DfC, might need more information about your health condition and how it affects you.​

They might ask for relevant information from your doctor, or any other relevant professional you tell them about.

DfC uses this information to process and to make a decision on your claim, or any mandatory reconsideration or appeal you make.

Any information that we ask you for is necessary to enable DfC to carry out its official duties. The law allows DfC to get, keep and use this information. 

If we ask your doctor, hospital consultant or other relevant professionals for information about your health condition and how it affects you, we will let them know that they can lawfully share this information with DfC.​

This is because we are asking for the information to help us carry out our official social security functions.​
